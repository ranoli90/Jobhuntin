# JobHuntin SEO Research Case Study
## Closed-Loop Simulation & Experimental Roadmap

This document outlines the methodology and results of the closed-loop SEO research conducted on the JobHuntin platform.

### 1. Research Objective
To simulate SEO performance from zero using unconventional visibility methods, discovery modeling, and automated authority siloing.

### 2. Methodology
The research was divided into three distinct phases:

#### Phase 1: Structural Audit
- **Metadata Management**: Implementation of a centralized `SEO.tsx` component to handle titles, meta tags, Open Graph, and JSON-LD.
- **Crawlability**: Configuration of `robots.txt` and a dynamic `sitemap.xml` generator to ensure 100% route discovery.
- **Semantic Signals**: Integration of Schema.org structured data for `SoftwareApplication` and `Product` entities.

#### Phase 2: Influence & Competitor Modeling
- **Topical Authority Hub**: Creation of the "AI Job Search Playbook" to establish semantic dominance in the automation niche.
- **Comparative Modeling**: Implementation of dynamic "Vs" pages to capture search intent from established competitors (Sorce, Simplify, Teal).
- **Link Graph Simulation**: Modeling internal link equity distribution to optimize authority flow to high-conversion nodes.

#### Phase 3: Experimental Roadmap
- **Programmatic Scaling**: Deployment of niche pages for localized role/city combinations.
- **Discovery Simulation**: Automated auditing of crawl depth and metadata integrity using `discovery-modeling.cjs`.
- **Traffic Simulation**: Predictive modeling of user behavior and its impact on search rankings using `traffic-simulation.cjs`.

### 3. Simulation Findings

| Metric | Simulated Result | Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Crawl Accessibility** | 100% | Full indexing of all programmatic nodes. |
